Hardware Components Guide for Robot Builds

Microcontrollers and Single Board Computers:
- Arduino Mega 2560: 54 digital I/O, good for simple robots. $15-25.
- Teensy 4.1: 600MHz Cortex-M7, excellent for real-time control. $30.
- STM32 Nucleo boards: Industrial-grade, CAN bus support. $15-50.
- Raspberry Pi 4/5: Linux-based, good for vision and high-level control. $35-80.
- NVIDIA Jetson Nano/Orin: GPU-accelerated for AI/ML inference. $99-499.
- OpenCR 1.0 (ROBOTIS): Designed for Dynamixel servos, ROS 2 compatible. $180.

Motor Drivers:
- L298N: Dual H-bridge, up to 2A per channel. Basic DC motor control. $3.
- DRV8825: Stepper driver, 1/32 microstepping, 2.2A. $5.
- TMC2209: Silent stepper driver, UART control, 2A. $8.
- ODrive v3.6: Dual BLDC driver, FOC control, encoder input. $120.
- VESC 6: Open-source BLDC driver, CAN bus, current sensing. $80.

Sensors:
- AS5600: Magnetic rotary encoder, 12-bit, I2C. $3.
- MPU6050: 6-axis IMU (accelerometer + gyroscope), I2C. $2.
- BNO055: 9-axis absolute orientation sensor, I2C. $25.
- VL53L0X: Time-of-flight distance sensor, 2m range. $8.
- Force Sensitive Resistor (FSR): Simple force measurement. $5.

Communication:
- UART/Serial: Simple point-to-point, up to 115200 baud.
- I2C: Multi-device bus, short distance, up to 400kHz.
- SPI: High-speed, full-duplex, good for IMUs and displays.
- CAN Bus: Robust multi-device, used in automotive and industrial robots.
- Ethernet: High bandwidth, used with ROS 2 for inter-device communication.
- Bluetooth/WiFi: Wireless control and telemetry.

Power:
- LiPo batteries: High energy density, 3.7V per cell (2S=7.4V, 3S=11.1V, 4S=14.8V).
- DC power supplies: Bench supplies for development (12V/24V, 5-30A).
- Voltage regulators: Buck converters for 5V/3.3V logic from motor voltage.

Motor Selection Guide for Robotics

Types of Motors:
- DC Brushed Motors: Simple, cheap, good for basic applications. Low efficiency.
- Brushless DC (BLDC): Higher efficiency, longer life, smoother operation. Used in drones and arms.
- Stepper Motors: Precise position control without feedback. NEMA 17/23 common in robotics.
- Servo Motors: Closed-loop with encoder. Best for robotic arms and precise positioning.
- Linear Actuators: Convert rotary to linear motion. Used for grippers and prismatic joints.

Key Specifications:
- Torque (Nm): Force × distance. Must exceed joint load with safety factor (1.5-3x).
- Speed (RPM): Rotational speed. Consider gear reduction for torque multiplication.
- Voltage: Operating voltage must match power supply and driver.
- Current: Peak and continuous current ratings for driver sizing.
- Encoder Resolution: CPR (counts per revolution) for position feedback.

Torque Calculation:
For a revolute joint: τ = m × g × L × SF
Where m = mass of links beyond joint, g = 9.81 m/s², L = distance to center of mass, SF = safety factor.

Common Motor Families:
- Dynamixel (ROBOTIS): Popular smart servos with built-in controller (MX, XM, XH series).
- NEMA stepper motors: Standardized frame sizes (NEMA 17 = 42mm, NEMA 23 = 57mm).
- Maxon motors: Premium brushless motors for precision robotics.
- ODrive-compatible BLDC: Custom servo systems using ODrive motor controllers.

Motor Driver Matching:
- Stepper: Use TMC2209, A4988, or DRV8825 drivers.
- BLDC: Use ODrive, VESC, or SimpleFOC-compatible drivers.
- Servo: Often integrated; external drivers for larger servos.
